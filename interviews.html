Professional Interviews

Interview #1: ERP Functional Analyst (SAP OTC), Ocean Spray

Career Journey

  • First degree in International Relations
  • Discovered interest in tech through hackathons
  • Switched into Information Systems
  • First job at consulting firm, learned SAP for orders and billing
  • Now at a food & beverage company supporting the “Order to Cash” process

A Day in the Life

  • Checks emails and tickets from sales/finance
  • Handles billing errors, pricing updates, reporting requests
  • Configures SAP to meet business needs
  • Runs tests to confirm updates work correctly
  • Creates training guides for end users
  • Documents changes and progress before day ends

Advice for Students

  • Understand business processes (orders, billing, payments) first
  • Practice writing clear notes and simple “user stories”

Insight

  • Most rewarding part is when updates save employees time or stress

Quote

“Do not worry about learning everything at once. Focus on how a simple order moves through a system. Once you understand that, the rest becomes easier.”

Interview #2: Financial Systems Analyst, Finance and Defense

Career Journey

  • Began career in U.S. Navy working with IT systems
  • Learned discipline and problem-solving under pressure
  • Studied math and statistics after service
  • Worked as Database Specialist → Financial Analyst
  • Now Financial Systems Analyst ensuring finance software runs correctly

A Day in the Life

  • Checks overnight system logs for errors and fixes issues immediately
  • Meets with finance managers about reporting needs
  • Writes SQL queries to pull accurate data
  • Tests reports to confirm numbers match expectations
  • Documents work and updates tickets
  • Some days are calm, others involve urgent troubleshooting

Advice for Students

  • Start with Excel and SQL — widely used and essential
  • Learn how projects are broken into smaller tasks (Agile mindset)
  • Consider certifications in Scrum or business analysis
  • Communication and trust-building are just as important as technical skills

Insight

  • Success comes from staying flexible and calm when priorities or regulations shift

Quote

“If you can explain a problem in simple terms and suggest a clear next step, people will want you on their team.”
